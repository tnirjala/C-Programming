A prosthetic leg (kritim khutta), also known as an artificial limb or a prosthetic limb, is a custom-made device designed to replace a missing or amputated leg. It is created to restore mobility and functionality to individuals who have lost their leg due to injury, disease, or congenital conditions. Prosthetic legs are typically made from lightweight and durable materials such as carbon fiber, titanium, or plastics. The design and complexity of the prosthetic leg can vary depending on the level of amputation and the specific needs of the individual.
