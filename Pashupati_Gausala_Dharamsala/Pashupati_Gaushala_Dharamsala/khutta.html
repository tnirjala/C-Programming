Krtittim-Khutta

At Shree Pashupati Gaushala Dharamashala, we provide free prosthetic legs (Krithim Khutta) to individuals who have undergone amputation. These prosthetic legs enable them to walk with a more natural and energy-efficient gait pattern, often eliminating the need for aids like crutches or walkers.


Once the wound from the amputation is stable and well-healed, individuals can be fitted for a prosthetic leg. If there are any after-amputation difficulties, the fitting process may be postponed.


It's important to note that the prosthetic legs (Krithim Khutta) provided at Shree Pashupati Gaushala Dharamashala are manufactured within the Dharamshala, using raw materials sourced from outside.


These prosthetic legs are available free of cost on every alternate Saturday. To obtain a prosthetic leg, individuals will need a recommendation letter from the ward office. For more information, please contact the following numbers: 9841857428, 9851023472.
